Stage 2: wait, notify, and notifyAll — coordinating threads on a monitor. Mutual exclusion stops threads from colliding. Condition signalling lets them coordinate — "wait until the queue is non-empty." The monitor provides wait, notify, and notifyAll, and you may only call them while holding that monitor.

The pattern: inside a synchronized block on the lock, while the queue is empty, call lock dot wait. wait releases the lock, parks the thread, and re-acquires the lock on wake before returning. Then remove and return. The producer, also synchronized on the same lock, adds the item and calls notifyAll.

Three rules everyone gets wrong. First: always wait in a while loop, never an if. On wake-up you must re-check the condition, because another thread may have raced in and changed it — and spurious wakeups are explicitly permitted by the spec, meaning wait can return without any notify at all. If you used an if, you'd proceed on a false assumption. Second: wait atomically releases the lock and parks, then re-acquires it before returning. That release is precisely what lets the producer get in — if wait held the lock while sleeping, you'd deadlock. Third: prefer notifyAll over notify unless you can prove all waiters are interchangeable. notify wakes one arbitrary waiter; if that waiter is waiting on a different condition, your needed waiter sleeps forever — a lost-wakeup, or missed-signal, bug.

This low-level pattern is the foundation of every blocking queue and bounded buffer. But you rarely write it by hand anymore. You reach for java.util.concurrent — Day 13's BlockingQueue — or the Condition objects in the next stage, which make the intent explicit and the bugs much harder to write.

So, two interview answers. Why must wait be in a loop? To re-check the condition after waking, because the state may have changed before you re-acquired the lock, and spurious wakeups are allowed. And notify versus notifyAll? notify wakes one arbitrary waiter, with the risk it's the wrong one and the right one starves; notifyAll wakes all, they re-check, and the right ones proceed. Default to notifyAll unless waiters are provably uniform.
