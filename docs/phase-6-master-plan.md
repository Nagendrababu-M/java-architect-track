## Week 21 — Behavioral, STAR, Narrative (Days 134–140)

### Day 134 — The Behavioral Interview at Staff/Architect Level
- **Core concepts:**
  - How behavioral interviews change at architect level: scope, ambiguity, leadership-without-authority
  - Common architect-specific themes: handling disagreement with senior engineers, deprecating old systems, making decisions with incomplete information, mentoring/scaling teams
  - The "Architect Filter" — interviewers screen for system thinkers, not just senior coders
- **Architect lens:** Your stories must show *systems thinking*, not just task completion
- **Interview signal:** Watch for "tell me about a time you disagreed with leadership" — this is a top-3 question
- **Resources:**
  - "Staff Engineer" by Will Larson (the canonical book for staff+ roles)
  - "Staff Engineer's Path" by Tanya Reilly
  - staffeng.com (free stories from real staff engineers)

### Day 135 — STAR Method Deep Dive
- **Core concepts:**
  - S (Situation): Context, 1-2 sentences
  - T (Task): What was your responsibility specifically
  - A (Action): What YOU did (not "the team did") — 60% of the story
  - R (Result): Quantified outcome
- **Common mistakes:** Spending too long on Situation, vague Actions, missing the Result
- **Architect twist:** Add "L (Learnings)" — what you'd do differently. Shows growth mindset.
- **Challenge:** Write 3 STAR stories from your trading work
- **Resources:**
  - Amazon Leadership Principles examples
  - "Cracking the PM Interview" — Gayle McDowell (behavioral chapter applies broadly)

### Day 136 — Your Story Bank: 12 Core Stories
- **Format:** Build out a story bank
- **The 12 themes every architect candidate needs stories for:**
  1. Most complex technical project
  2. Time you disagreed with a senior person
  3. Failure / mistake and what you learned
  4. Influencing without authority
  5. Mentoring a junior engineer
  6. Making a decision with incomplete data
  7. Deprecating / killing a system or project
  8. Cross-functional collaboration win
  9. Performance optimization win (quantified)
  10. Production incident you led
  11. Pushing back on a bad requirement
  12. Time you championed a controversial choice that paid off
- **Deliverable:** 1-page Markdown story bank with 12 STAR stories
- **Architect lens:** Most candidates have 3-4 stories; architects have 12+ on demand

### Day 137 — Behavioral Practice: Conflict, Leadership, Influence
- **Format:** Practice 3-5 questions out loud, recorded
- **Sample questions:**
  - "Tell me about a time you had to convince a senior engineer to change a decision"
  - "Describe a project where you led without formal authority"
  - "Walk me through a difficult technical disagreement and how you resolved it"
- **Self-review criteria:** Length (90 sec - 2 min ideal), structure (STAR clear), specificity (concrete numbers), self-awareness

### Day 138 — Behavioral Practice: Failure, Ambiguity, Trade-offs
- **Format:** Same as Day 137, different theme
- **Sample questions:**
  - "Tell me about your biggest professional failure"
  - "Describe a time you made a decision without enough information"
  - "Tell me about a project that didn't go as planned"
- **Architect-specific:** These probe humility and judgment under uncertainty

### Day 139 — Salary Negotiation Fundamentals
- **Core concepts:**
  - Total comp components: base, bonus, equity (RSUs, options, vesting cliff), sign-on, relocation
  - The "don't disclose first" rule
  - Bands by level (Senior, Staff, Principal) at top Indian companies + FAANG India
  - Competing offers as leverage
  - The "I'm worth X because Y" framing
- **Architect lens:** Architects are senior enough to negotiate seriously; juniors aren't
- **Resources:**
  - "Salary Negotiation" — Patrick McKenzie ("patio11") classic blog post
  - levels.fyi (data on actual comp by company/level)

### Day 140 — Resume & LinkedIn for Architect Roles
- **Core concepts:**
  - Architect resumes emphasize *impact* and *scope*, not tools
  - "Designed", "Led", "Architected" — verbs that signal level
  - Quantified achievements (latency reduced X%, scale supported Y RPS, cost saved Z lakhs)
  - The 3 must-haves: scope of systems owned, scale handled, business outcomes
  - LinkedIn: same principles, plus profile photo, banner, "Open to Work" toggle
- **Architect lens:** Hiring managers spend 30 seconds; lead with the strongest signal
- **Deliverable:** Refreshed resume + LinkedIn

## Week 22 — Full Mock Interviews (Days 141–147)

### Day 141 — Full Mock: Coding Interview
- **Format:** 45-min mock with realistic interviewer (use Pramp, Interviewing.io, or a friend)
- **Pattern:** 1 medium-hard problem, expected to solve in 30-35 min with discussion
- **Self-grade rubric:**
  - Asked clarifying questions before coding? (Y/N)
  - Stated time/space complexity? (Y/N)
  - Identified pattern in <3 min? (Y/N)
  - Code compiled mentally / would run? (Y/N)
  - Discussed test cases / edge cases? (Y/N)
  - Discussed extensions / improvements? (Y/N)
- **After:** 15-min reflection — what would you redo?

### Day 142 — Full Mock: LLD Interview
- **Format:** 45-min mock LLD (parking lot, splitwise, elevator, or chess)
- **Pattern:**
  - 5 min requirements
  - 10 min class design
  - 15 min core implementation
  - 10 min concurrency/extensibility discussion
  - 5 min trade-off review
- **Self-grade rubric:**
  - Drove the requirements gathering? (Y/N)
  - Used SOLID consciously? (Y/N)
  - Applied GoF patterns by name? (Y/N)
  - Discussed concurrency? (Y/N)
  - Talked about testing? (Y/N)

### Day 143 — Full Mock: HLD Interview
- **Format:** 45-min HLD (pick from Twitter, Uber, WhatsApp, Instagram, Tinder)
- **Pattern:**
  - 5 min requirements + scoping
  - 5 min capacity estimation
  - 15 min high-level architecture
  - 15 min deep-dive on 1-2 components
  - 5 min bottleneck + scaling discussion
- **Self-grade rubric:**
  - Asked scoping questions? (Y/N)
  - Did napkin math? (Y/N)
  - Discussed trade-offs explicitly? (Y/N)
  - Identified the right deep-dive areas? (Y/N)
  - Defended choices with reasoning? (Y/N)

### Day 144 — Full Mock: Behavioral Interview
- **Format:** 45-min behavioral round, 6-8 questions
- **Themes:** Mix of leadership, conflict, failure, decision-making
- **Self-grade rubric:**
  - All stories <2 min? (Y/N)
  - STAR structure clear? (Y/N)
  - Specific numbers / concrete details? (Y/N)
  - Self-awareness / learnings shown? (Y/N)
  - Different stories for each question (no recycling)? (Y/N)

### Day 145 — Full Loop Day 1: Coding + LLD
- **Format:** Back-to-back, simulate real loop
- **Coding:** 45 min
- **5 min break**
- **LLD:** 45 min
- **Reflection:** 30 min — energy management, transitioning between styles, mental fatigue

### Day 146 — Full Loop Day 2: HLD + Behavioral
- **Format:** Same simulation
- **HLD:** 45 min
- **5 min break**
- **Behavioral:** 45 min
- **Reflection:** 30 min — note any answers that felt weak, plan follow-ups

### Day 147 — Phase 6 Synthesis & Curriculum Completion
- **Format:** Retrospective + forward planning
- **Reflection:**
  - What are your 3 strongest areas? Lead with those.
  - What are your 3 weakest? Plan continued practice.
  - Which companies match your strengths?
- **Final deliverable:** A 1-page "Interview-Ready Summary" — your elevator pitch, strongest stories, target compensation, target companies
- **Architect lens:** You've completed 155 days of architect training. Now go execute the offer process.

## Phase 6 Exit Criteria

By Day 147 you should:

- Have a 12-story behavioral bank ready
- Deliver any STAR story in under 2 minutes, on demand
- Comfortably navigate a 45-min mock in any format (coding, LLD, HLD, behavioral)
- Know your target comp range and how to negotiate it
- Have a refreshed resume and LinkedIn
- Feel calm walking into a real architect-level loop

## Post-Phase-6: What's Next

When Day 147 ends, you've completed the curriculum. The realistic next steps:

1. **Apply** to 15-30 architect-level roles
2. **Continue mocks** weekly until you have offers
3. **Maintain skills** — keep solving 2-3 LeetCode mediums/week, doing 1 system design/week
4. **Build a public portfolio** — blog posts on architecture decisions, GitHub projects
5. **Network** — staff-level moves often happen via referral, not application

## The Honest Reality Check

22 weeks is aggressive. Some days will slip. Some weeks will feel useless. Some concepts won't click the first time.

That's normal. The curriculum is designed to be revisited — master plans stay in the repo, progress is tracked locally, and individual lesson HTMLs can be regenerated for revision.

If you complete Phases 1-3 and want to interview *now*, you can. The remaining phases add depth but aren't strictly required for many roles.
